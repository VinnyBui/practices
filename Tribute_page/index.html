About

Kim Ji-soo is a South Korean singer and actress. In 2011, Jisoo joined YG Entertainment through auditions as a trainee. In 2015, she made a cameo appearance in the KBS2 drama The Producers with label-mates Sandara Park of 2NE1 and Kang Seung-yoon of Winner and featured in several advertisements, including those for Samsonite, Smart Uniform, LG Electronics, and Nikon. She debut under YG Entertainment as one of the four members of Blackpink in August (2016) with the album Square One. It includes the songs Boombayah and Whistle debuted atop the Gaon Digital Chart while "Boombayah" became the group's first number-one hit on the Billboard World Digital Song Sales chart. With the sucess of Blackpink over the years, she began to venture into acting in 2019 with a short cameo appearance in the tvN fantasy drama Arthdal Chronicles. On August 18, 2020 YG Entertainment confirmed Jisoo would star in her first fixed role as a lead actress in the upcoming JTBC drama Snowdrop

Kim Ji-soo was born on January 3, 1995, in Gunpo, Gyeonggi, South Korea and has an older brother and sister. As a child, she played basketball and attended taekwondo classes. She was also a fan of TVXQ as a child. Jisoo attended high school at the School of Performing Arts Seoul. In 11th grade, she start-off drama club at her school and took larger experience through auditions. Her native language is Korean but she also took the time to learn to speak Chinese, Japanese and English. Despite being an idol and actress at the same time, she used to dream of becoming a painter and writer beforehand. Prior to her debut, Jisoo lived with her parents, sister, brother, and grandparents.
